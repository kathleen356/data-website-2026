6.1 Data Range 📊

How spread out is your data? Today we learn to find out — using just one subtraction!

🎯 Learning Intention

We are learning to identify a range of data.

The Rule
Range = Maximum − Minimum
The range tells us how spread out a data set is.

⭐ Success Criteria — I can…

  • identify the maximum and minimum values in a data set
  • calculate the range using maximum − minimum
  • explain what the range tells me about spread
  • compare two data sets and say which is more consistent

💡 Click each one to tick it off as you master it!

🔍 See it in action

A group of Year 5 students counted how many books they read in Term 1. Press the buttons in order and watch what happens!

The data: 4, 9, 2, 12, 7, 5, 15, 3 books

👣 How to work it out — step by step

Same data, written the way you should set it out in your book. Click "Next step".

1 Find the  MAXIMUM  (highest) value 15
2 Find the  MINIMUM  (lowest) value 2
3 Subtract: Maximum − Minimum 15 − 2 = 13
✓ The range is 13 books

🧠 Remember:

  • Always identify max and min before subtracting
  • Range is always a positive number (max − min, never min − max)
  • A bigger range = more spread out. A smaller range = more consistent.

🟢 Must Complete Work — Find the Range GREEN
  1. What is the range of this data set?
    3, 8, 1, 6, 4
    ✔ 8 − 1 = 7
  2. A student recorded her daily screen time in minutes:
    45, 90, 30, 75, 60
    What is the range?
    ✔ 90 − 30 = 60 minutes
  3. Five students scored these points in a quiz:
    12, 7, 18, 5, 14
    What is the range?
    ✔ 18 − 5 = 13 points
  4. The temperatures on five mornings were:
    9°C, 14°C, 6°C, 11°C, 16°C
    Find the range.
    ✔ 16°C − 6°C = 10°C
  5. A pet store counted the number of fish sold each day:
    22, 35, 17, 41, 28
    What is the range?
    ✔ 41 − 17 = 24 fish
  6. Find the range:
    100, 250, 175, 300, 125
    ✔ 300 − 100 = 200
🟠 Find the Range and Explain AMBER
  1. A class recorded how many minutes they spent reading each night:
    20, 45, 10, 55, 30, 40, 15
    Find the range and write one sentence explaining what it tells you about the data.
    ✔ 55 − 10 = 45 minutes. The reading times are very spread out — some students read 45 minutes more per night than others.
  2. Two groups of students completed a sprint.
    Group A times (seconds): 14, 16, 13, 15, 17
    Group B times (seconds): 11, 19, 14, 21, 10
    Find the range for both groups. Which group was more consistent?
    ✔ Group A: 17 − 13 = 4 seconds. Group B: 21 − 10 = 11 seconds. Group A was more consistent because its range is smaller.
  3. The number of people who visited a library over 7 days was:
    112, 98, 143, 87, 156, 102, 119
    Find the range and explain what it tells you about visitor numbers.
    ✔ 156 − 87 = 69 people. Visitor numbers varied a lot from day to day — busy days had up to 69 more visitors than quiet days.
  4. A gardener recorded how many tomatoes she picked each week:
    15, 23, 8, 31, 19, 27
    Find the range. Then explain whether this range tells you exactly how many tomatoes she usually picks.
    ✔ 31 − 8 = 23 tomatoes. No — the range only shows the spread between the best and worst weeks. It does not tell you the usual (typical) amount she picks.
🔴 Putting Knowledge in Context RED
  1. A data set has a range of 24. The minimum value is 13. What is the maximum value? Explain how you worked this out.
    ✔ Maximum = minimum + range = 13 + 24 = 37. Since range = max − min, we can rearrange it to max = min + range.
  2. A sports coach wants to know if her team's performance is becoming more consistent over three weeks.
    Week 1 scores: 45, 67, 52, 71, 49
    Week 2 scores: 55, 62, 58, 68, 53
    Week 3 scores: 59, 64, 61, 66, 60
    Calculate the range for each week and explain what the trend shows.
    ✔ Week 1: 71 − 45 = 26. Week 2: 68 − 53 = 15. Week 3: 66 − 59 = 7. The range gets smaller each week, so the team's scores are becoming more consistent over time.

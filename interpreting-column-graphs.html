1 Single vs double column graphs

A column graph (bar graph) uses the height of columns to show how much of something there is. There are two types:

Single column graph — one column per category.
Double column graph — two columns per category, so you can compare two groups. Needs a key.

Notice the double graph needs a key so you know which colour is which group.

4 Three layers of thinking about data

Good data detectives don't just read numbers — they compare and conclude. When you look at any graph, work through these three layers:

1 · See

What can I see straight away? Read values off the graph.

2 · Find out

What can I work out? Compare and combine the data.

3 · Conclude

What can I conclude? Spot patterns and draw inferences.

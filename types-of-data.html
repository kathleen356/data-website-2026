1 The data family tree

Every piece of data has a family. To find out where it belongs, ask two questions: 1) Is it a word or a number? Then 2) what kind?

DATA

🏷️ Categorical

Words, labels or groups. You can't do maths with them.

NominalNo natural order. Eye colour, favourite sport.
OrdinalCategories you can rank. Movie ratings, sizes.

🔢 Numerical

Numbers you can count or measure. You can do maths with them.

DiscreteWhole-number jumps. Students in a class, goals scored.
ContinuousAny value on a scale. Weight, height, time.

2 Categorical data: nominal vs ordinal

Nominal data has no natural order — you can't say one label is "more" than another. Ordinal data can be ranked from lowest to highest, even though it's still words.

Drag each card into the bin where it belongs.

A survey asks students to pick their favourite house group (Red, Blue, Green, Yellow). Is this nominal or ordinal?
💡 There's no order to house colours — none is "higher" than another — so it's nominal.

3 Numerical data: discrete vs continuous

Discrete data is counted — it jumps in whole numbers with nothing sensible in between (you can't have 2.5 pets). Continuous data is measured — it can take any value on a scale, including the bits in between.

Drag each card into the bin where it belongs.

A stopwatch records how long it takes a runner to finish a lap. Is this discrete or continuous?
💡 Time can be any value — 21.4 seconds, 21.43 seconds — so it's continuous.
